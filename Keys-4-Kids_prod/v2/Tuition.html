Tuition Policies

Continuing Students

A complete semester payment (or $100 deposit per student) is due on May 10 for Fall Semester & December 10 for Spring Semester. An enrollment form submitted without deposit is considered incomplete and the student will not be assigned to a class or lesson time. The remaining balance is due by August 10 for Fall Semester & January 10 for Spring Semester. Email reminders will be sent until the tuition is paid in full. Tuition bills that are 10 days overdue are assessed a $25.00 late fee. Students with tuition payments 20 days overdue are not eligible to attend classes and will receive a discontinuation notice.

New Students

Payment is due at the time of enrollment. An enrollment form submitted without deposit is considered incomplete, and the student will not be assigned to a class or lesson time.

Tuition Discounts

Family Discount: $50 off the tuition is given for families with 2 or more enrolled students (assumes full 18-week semester enrollment for each child).
Referral Discount: Refer a friend and you both receive $50 tuition credit. Tuition credit will apply to the first semester after the new studentâ€™s enrollment enrollment (assumes full 18-week semester enrollment).
Music Immersion Discount: $50 off the tuition for students enrolled in multiple programs (assumes full 18-week semester enrollment).

Private Student Book Deposits

Keys for Kids faculty members handle the ordering and purchasing of music and other materials for private PIANO students to avoid delays in acquiring diverse materials needed for private lessons. Private PIANO students are required to make an initial $50 book deposit once per year. A record will be kept of all music expenses and other miscellaneous fees. When the balance falls below $10, you will be asked to bring your account up to a credit balance of $50. If a student should terminate lessons, any unused deposits in this account will be refunded.

Credit Card Payments

Payment with a credit card will incur an additional processing fee of 3%.
